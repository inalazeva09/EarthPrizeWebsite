Overall Product / Idea

Our project is an algae-based air purification system designed for healthcare environments. The system uses algae to help cleanse the air by absorbing carbon dioxide and supporting cleaner indoor conditions.

Sensors monitor air quality, temperature, and carbon dioxide levels. When pollution levels increase, the system automatically activates. LED lighting mimics sunlight to allow the algae to remain active through photosynthesis, while fans circulate air through the purifier.

Expansion Plan

Our long-term goal is to introduce this purifier into hospitals and clinics where air quality is especially important. Healthcare facilities constantly seek safer and healthier environments, making them ideal locations for this system.

With further development and testing, the purifier could be produced on a larger scale and implemented in multiple medical settings, including waiting areas and patient spaces.

Using algae as a renewable resource supports sustainability while exploring alternatives to conventional purification systems.

About Us

We are students interested in solving real-world problems through innovative ideas. Our focus on air quality and environmental sustainability inspired this project.

We chose this topic because hospitals are places of healing, and we wanted to explore how nature-inspired solutions could contribute to healthier indoor environments.
